AR-Enhanced Reading

BRIDGE BETWEEN PHYSICAL AND DIGITAL Use physical books as markers to project annotations, diagrams, and scholarly notes alongside printed text. ARCore (Android) and ARKit (iOS) enable overlay of digital knowledge on material substrates.

How AR-Enhanced Reading Works

1. SCAN PHYSICAL BOOK: Point your device camera at the book's cover or specific pages marked as AR-enabled.

2. RETRIEVE ANNOTATIONS: The app recognizes the book and retrieves relevant scholarly annotations, diagrams, and commentary from the Monastery archive.

3. PROJECT OVERLAY: Digital content is projected alongside the physical text, creating an augmented reading experience that preserves the materiality of the book while enhancing comprehension.

4. CONTRIBUTE ANNOTATIONS: Scholars can upload their own annotations and diagrams to be shared with future readers of the same physical text.

Technology Requirements

  • ‚Ä¢ ARCore-compatible Android device (Android 7.0+)
  • ‚Ä¢ ARKit-compatible iOS device (iPhone 6S or newer)
  • ‚Ä¢ Camera access for book recognition
  • ‚Ä¢ Physical books with ISBN or visual markers
PRESERVATION PHILOSOPHY: AR enhances but never replaces physical books. Digital annotations serve as supplementary scholarship, ensuring knowledge remains accessible even without technology through the printed word.
